What Is BMI?


  • Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure that uses height and weight to estimate body fat in adults. It is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters.

  • While BMI is a useful screening tool, it does not directly measure body fat and may not be accurate for all individuals, particularly those with high muscle mass, older adults, or certain ethnic groups. It is often used alongside other assessments to evaluate an individual's overall health.


What Are Different Types Of BMI?


  • Body Mass Index (BMI) categories are used to classify individuals based on their weight relative to their height.
    Here are the different types of BMI categories:

    1. Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
    2. Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
    3. Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
    4. Obesity Class 1 (Moderate): BMI 30 to 34.9
    5. Obesity Class 2 (Severe): BMI 35 to 39.9
    6. Obesity Class 3 (Very severe or morbid): BMI 40 and above

How To Calculate BMI?

  • To calculate Body Mass Index (BMI), you can use the following formula:

    • BMI= weight(kg)/(height(cm)/100)^2
